💬 General Questions
What is Yakeen International?

Yakeen International is a licensed overseas manpower recruitment agency based in Bangladesh. We connect skilled, semi-skilled, and general workers with verified international employers across the Middle East, Asia Pacific, and Europe. We are a sister concern of Pigeon Overseas (RL-0377) and are certified by BMET and BAIRA.
Is Yakeen International a licensed agency?
+
Yes. We are a fully licensed recruitment agency operating under the Bangladesh Government's Bureau of Manpower, Employment and Training (BMET) framework. We are also a member of the Bangladesh Association of International Recruiting Agencies (BAIRA). You can verify our credentials through these government bodies.

📋 The Application Process
How do I apply for a job abroad through Yakeen International?
+
It's simple:
  1. Browse vacancies on our website or visit our office.
  2. Register your interest via our contact form or in person.
  3. Submit your documents — NID, Passport, any trade certificates.
  4. Attend a trade test to verify your skills.
  5. Medical check-up at an approved center.
  6. Visa processing & BMET clearance — we handle this for you.
  7. Pre-departure orientation and then you're off!
How long does the entire process take?
+
The timeline varies by destination country and visa type, but generally:

Middle East (KSA, UAE): 4–8 weeks
Asia Pacific (Japan, Malaysia): 8–16 weeks
Europe (Portugal, Norway, Italy, etc.): 12–20 weeks

These timelines depend on document readiness, medical results, and employer confirmation. We process as fast as legally possible.
What documents do I need to apply?
+
Basic documents required for most destinations:
  • ✅ Valid Passport (minimum 2 years validity remaining)
  • ✅ National ID Card (NID)
  • ✅ Recent passport-size photographs
  • ✅ Educational / trade certificates (if applicable)
  • ✅ Police clearance certificate
  • ✅ Medical fitness certificate (from approved center)

Additional documents may be required depending on the destination country and specific job type. We will guide you through everything.
What is the BMET Smart Card and do I need it?
+
Yes — the BMET Smart Card is mandatory for all Bangladeshi workers going abroad legally. It is issued by the Bureau of Manpower, Employment and Training (BMET) and confirms that your migration was registered with the government.

Without it, your migration is considered illegal. We handle the BMET clearance process for every worker we deploy — at no additional hidden cost.
What is Pre-Departure Orientation (PDO)?
+
Pre-Departure Orientation is a briefing session we provide to all workers before they fly. It covers:
  • 🗺️ Culture and customs of the destination country
  • ⚖️ Local labor laws and your rights as a worker
  • 🏥 Safety regulations and emergency contacts
  • 💵 How to send money home (remittance)
  • 📞 What to do if problems arise abroad

PDO is included in our service — no extra charge.

What should I expect when working in Saudi Arabia?
+
Saudi Arabia is one of the top destinations for Bangladeshi workers. Here's what to expect:

Work Culture: Professional, punctual, and structured. Friday is the official day off. Dress modestly.

Labor Law: KSA labor law protects workers. You are entitled to a contract, rest days, accommodation, and medical care.

Kafala System: Your employer sponsors your visa. Ensure your contract matches your verbal agreement before signing.

Salary: Usually paid monthly in SAR (Saudi Riyal). You can send money home via bank transfer or money exchange services.

We cover all of this in our Pre-Departure Orientation for KSA-bound workers.
What should I know about working in Japan?
+
Japan has strict workplace standards and cultural norms:

Punctuality: Extremely important. Being late — even by a few minutes — is considered very disrespectful.

Work Ethic: Japanese workplaces value discipline, teamwork, and attention to detail highly.

Language: Basic Japanese phrases will help you significantly. We advise workers to learn numbers, greetings, and safety terms before departure.

Specified Skilled Worker (SSW) Visa: Japan requires passing a skills test and Japanese Language test for most job categories.

We provide preparation guidance for Japan-bound workers in our PDO.
🛡️ Avoiding Visa Scams
🚨
Important: Bangladesh's manpower industry has serious fraud problems. Read this section carefully — it could save you from losing your savings or your safety.
How do I avoid visa scams in Bangladesh?
+
Visa fraud is unfortunately very common. Here are the key warning signs to watch out for:

🚩 Red Flags — Avoid if you see these:
  • Agent demands large cash payment before showing any job offer
  • Job offer is via WhatsApp only — no formal document
  • Salary promised seems unrealistically high
  • Agency has no BMET license or BAIRA membership
  • You are asked to travel on a tourist visa for a "work" trip
  • No written contract before payment
  • Pressure to "pay now or lose the slot"
✅ Safe Signs — What a legitimate agency does:
  • Shows you a verified demand letter from the employer
  • Provides a written contract before taking payment
  • Has a physical office and verifiable license
  • Does not rush or pressure you
  • Is registered with BMET and BAIRA
  • Processes your BMET Smart Card legally
What is a "free visa" and should I trust it?
+
A "free visa" typically refers to a visa where the employer covers the visa fees — this can be legitimate for some countries and job types.

However, many fraudulent agents use the term "free visa" to attract victims and then charge hidden fees later, or provide tourist visas instead of proper work visas.

Always verify:
  • ✅ Is the visa a proper work visa (not tourist)?
  • ✅ Is there a signed employment contract?
  • ✅ Does the demand letter match the job and salary offered?
  • ✅ Is BMET clearance being processed?

If in doubt, contact BMET or BAIRA to verify the agency's legitimacy.
What should I do if I've been scammed by a recruitment agency?
+
If you believe you have been defrauded:

1. File a complaint with BMET:
Bureau of Manpower, Employment and Training
Hotline: 16135 | Website: bmet.gov.bd

2. Contact BAIRA:
Bangladesh Association of International Recruiting Agencies
They can help resolve disputes with member agencies.

3. File a police report:
Go to your nearest police station and file a General Diary (GD) with all evidence — receipts, contracts, WhatsApp messages.

4. Contact us:
Even if you didn't use our agency, we are happy to advise you on the right steps to take.
